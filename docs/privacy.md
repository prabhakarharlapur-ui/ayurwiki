# Privacy Policy

**Last updated:** March 26, 2026

Ayurwiki ("we", "us", or "our") operates the Ayurwiki mobile application and the website at [ayurwiki.org](https://ayurwiki.org). This Privacy Policy explains what information we access, collect, and use, how we handle health-related content, and your rights regarding that information.

## Health-Related Content

### Nature of the Content

Ayurwiki is an **educational reference application** that provides general information about Ayurveda, including descriptions of herbs, traditional medicines, yoga practices, and Ayurvedic concepts. This content falls under the category of **Health** information.

### How Health Content Is Accessed and Used

- The app **downloads and displays** educational articles about Ayurvedic herbs, medicines, and practices from the ayurwiki.org knowledge base
- This health-related content is **general reference material** and is stored locally on your device for offline reading
- You may **bookmark** health-related articles for personal reference
- You may **search** through health-related content using the app's search feature
- Health content is **read-only** — the app does not modify, personalise, or tailor health content based on your personal health information

### What We Do NOT Do with Health Data

- We do **not** collect, store, or process any **personal health information** from users
- We do **not** track which health articles you read, search for, or bookmark — all reading activity stays on your device
- We do **not** provide **medical diagnoses, treatment plans, or personalised health recommendations**
- We do **not** share any health-related usage data with third parties
- We do **not** use health content access patterns for advertising or profiling

### Medical Disclaimer

The health-related content provided by Ayurwiki is for **educational and informational purposes only**. It is not intended as a substitute for professional medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment. Always seek the advice of a qualified healthcare provider with any questions you may have regarding a medical condition. Never disregard professional medical advice or delay seeking it because of information accessed through this app.

## Information We Collect

### GitHub Account Information (Optional)

When you choose to contribute edits through our app or website, you sign in using your GitHub account via OAuth. Through this process, we receive:

- **GitHub username** (login)
- **Display name** (if set on your GitHub profile)
- **Profile picture URL**
- **OAuth access token** (for authentication only)

We do **not** request access to your repositories, email address, or any private GitHub data. We use the minimal OAuth scope (public profile only).

### Locally Stored Data

The Ayurwiki app stores the following data locally on your device:

- **Article content** — health-related educational articles downloaded from ayurwiki.org for offline reading
- **Bookmarks** — your saved articles (stored locally only)
- **Drafts** — your in-progress edit suggestions (stored locally only)
- **Authentication credentials** — your GitHub OAuth token (stored in secure device storage)

This data stays on your device and is not transmitted to any server, except when you explicitly submit an edit.

### Data We Do Not Collect

- We do **not** collect analytics or usage data
- We do **not** use tracking pixels, advertising SDKs, or third-party analytics
- We do **not** collect your location, contacts, or device identifiers
- We do **not** collect your email address
- We do **not** collect any personal health or medical information
- We do **not** track your reading history, search queries, or bookmarks

## How We Use Your Information

Your GitHub profile information (when provided) is used solely to:

1. **Attribute your contributions** — when you submit an edit suggestion, your GitHub username is included in the GitHub Issue so the community can credit your contribution
2. **Authenticate your session** — your OAuth token verifies your identity when submitting edits

Health-related article content is used solely to:

1. **Display educational information** — articles are shown to you for reading
2. **Enable offline access** — articles are stored on your device so you can read them without an internet connection
3. **Enable search** — article content is indexed locally on your device for full-text search

## Data Sharing

### What We Share

- **Edit contributions** — when you submit an edit, your GitHub username and the edit content are posted as a public GitHub Issue on our open-source repository
- **No other data is shared** with any third party

### What We Do NOT Share

- We do **not** share your reading activity, bookmarks, or search queries with anyone
- We do **not** share health-related usage data with advertisers, analytics companies, or data brokers
- We do **not** sell any user data

## Third-Party Services

### GitHub

We use GitHub OAuth for authentication and GitHub Issues for contribution management. When you sign in, you interact with GitHub's services. Please review [GitHub's Privacy Statement](https://docs.github.com/en/site-policy/privacy-policies/github-general-privacy-statement).

### Cloudflare

Our API is hosted on Cloudflare Workers. Cloudflare may process request metadata (IP address, headers) as part of standard web infrastructure. Please review [Cloudflare's Privacy Policy](https://www.cloudflare.com/privacypolicy/).

## Data Retention

- **On your device:** Data persists until you uninstall the app or log out. Logging out removes your authentication credentials. Uninstalling the app removes all locally stored data, including downloaded articles, bookmarks, and drafts.
- **On GitHub:** Submitted edit suggestions are created as GitHub Issues on our public repository. These are public and persist as part of the project's open-source record.
- **On our servers:** We do not maintain a database of user accounts or personal information. No user data is stored on our servers beyond what is transiently processed during an API request.

## Your Rights

You can at any time:

- **Log out** from the app to remove your stored credentials
- **Clear bookmarks and drafts** within the app
- **Uninstall** the app to remove all locally stored data, including health content, bookmarks, and search history
- **Revoke access** by visiting [GitHub Settings > Applications](https://github.com/settings/applications) and revoking Ayurwiki's OAuth access

## Children's Privacy

Ayurwiki does not knowingly collect information from children under 13. Our service relies on GitHub accounts, which require users to be at least 13 years old.
