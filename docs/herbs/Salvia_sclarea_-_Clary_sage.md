# Salvia sclarea - Clary sage

[TOC]

![*Salvia sclarea*, *clary sage*](../images/Salvia_sclarea3.jpg)
**Salvia sclarea** is a berennial or short-lived herbaceous perennial in the genus Salvia. It is native to the northern Mediterranean Basin, along with some areas in north Africa and Central Asia. The plant has a lengthy history as a medicinal herb, and is currently grown for its essential oil.
## Uses
Acne, Boils, Dandruff, Hair loss, Inflamed conditions, Oily skin, Opthalmia, Wrinkles, Ulcers.

## Parts Used
Flowers, Leaves.

## Chemical Composition
linalool (an alcohol) and acetic acid (an acid) combine to become linalyl acetate

## Identification
### Leaf
Simple, Spreading mass, Foliage color is medium green Mostly green but varies toward gray

### Flower
Unisexual, 2-4cm long, Violet, white, 5, The pink to purple floral bracts remain showy after the white to mauve corollas fade and flower stalks with upright spikes

### Fruit
7–10 mm (0.28–0.4 in.) long pome, Clearly grooved lengthwise, Lowest hooked hairs aligned towards crown, With hooked hairs

## How to plant/cultivate
Grow in light, moderately fertile, humus-rich, moist but well-drained soil. Best in full sun but can tolerate light partial shade.

## Commonly seen growing in areas
Tall grasslands, Meadows, Borders of forests and fields.
