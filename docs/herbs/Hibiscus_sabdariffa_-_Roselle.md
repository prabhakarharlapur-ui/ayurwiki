# Hibiscus sabdariffa - Roselle

[TOC]

![*Roselle*, *Pundi*](../images/Roselle,_Hibiscus_sabdariffa,_2014_01.JPG)
**Roselle** is a species of Hibiscus. It is native to West Africa. It is used for the production of bast fibre and as an infusion, in which it may be known as carcade.
## Uses
Common cold, Weakness, Snakebites, Curing liver disorders, Skin eruptions, Blotches, Pimples, Diarrhea, Sore throats.

### Food
Hibiscus sabdariffa can be used in Food. Leaves are cooked as vegetable, fruits eaten raw.

## Parts Used
Sepal, Flowers, Roots, Seeds.

## Chemical Composition
Per 100 g, the fruit contains 49 calories, 84.5% H2O, 1.9 g protein, 0.1 g fat, 12.3 g total carbohydrate, 2.3 g fiber, 1.2 g ash, 1.72 mg Ca, 57 mg P, 2.9 mg Fe, 300 mg b-carotene equivalent

## Habit

## Identification
### Leaf
Simple, Leaves are usually alternate, 3 to 5 in (7.5-12.5 cm) long, green with reddish veins and long or short petioles

### Flower
Unisexual, 10–12 mm long, Yellow and red, 5, Flowers are axillary, solitary, regular, pedicellate

### Fruit
Dehiscent, 18–20 mm long, Fruit are 5-valved, non-fleshy, bright red ovoid capsule, With hooked hairs, 3-4 seeds

## Mode of Propagation
Seeds, Cuttings.

## How to plant/cultivate
Soil preparation should be deep, about 20 cm, and thorough. Seed, 11–22 kg/ha depending upon the soil. Roselle is available from October to December.

## Commonly seen growing in areas
Temperate area, Tropical area, Subtropical area
