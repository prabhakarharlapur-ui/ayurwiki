# Viburnum tinus - Laurustinus viburnum

[TOC]

![*Viburnum tinus*, *laurestine*](../images/Viburnum_February_2008-1.jpg)
**Viburnum tinus** is a species of flowering plant in the family Adoxaceae, native to the Mediterranean area of Europe and North Africa. Laurus signifies the leaves' similarities to bay laurel.
## Uses
Nausea, Vomiting, Ulcers, Dysentery, Dysmenorrhoea, Uterine colic, Hemorrhage, Diarrhea, Sore throats

## Parts Used
Fruits, Leaves.

## Chemical Composition
Methyl (2R-3-α-L-rhamnopyranosyloxy)glycerate, methyl (3R-4-α-L-rhamnopyranosyloxy-3-hydroxy)butanoate, bridelionoside B, (6S,7E,9R)-roseoside, linarionoside A, 3,7,11-trimethyl-1,6-dodecadien-3,10,11-triol, (+)-8-hydroxylinalool, β-sitosterol and daucosterol.

## Identification
### Leaf
Simple, Ovate–elliptic, Shiny dark green leaves are evergreen and similar in shape to those of bay laurel

### Flower
Bisexual, 2-4cm long, Pinkish-white, 5-20, Flowers Season is June - August

### Fruit
7–10 mm, Laurustinus fruit by Wendy Cutler, With hooked hairs, -

## Mode of Propagation
Seeds, Layering.

## How to plant/cultivate
Landscape Uses:Border, Massing, Screen, Specimen. An easily grown plant, succeeding in both acid and alkaline soils but it is ill-adapted for poor soils and dry situations.

## Commonly seen growing in areas
Luxuriant type of macchia vegetation, Undergrowth in woods, Near the sea region.
