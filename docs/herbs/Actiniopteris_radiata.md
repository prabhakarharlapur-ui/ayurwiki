# Actiniopteris radiata

[TOC]

![](../images/Actiniopteris_radiata-1-ghatroad-yercaud-salem-India.JPG)
**Actiniopteris radiata** is a small fern growing upto 10 - 15cm tall from a short, creeping rhizome. The fronds have fan-shaped leaves.  The plant is sometimes harvested from the wild for local medicinal use. It is often grown as an ornamental in gardens.

## Uses
Fever, Heamorrhages, Bronchitis, Colic pain, Sprain, Injuries.

## Parts Used
Whole Plant

## Chemical Composition
It contains 37 compounds, out of 38, were identified with the combination of fatty acids, alkanes, triterpenoids and sterols. 9,12,15-octadecatrien-1-ol (18.7 %), Hexa decanoic acid (10.8 %), Hepatacosane (8.3 %), α-amyrin (7.0%), β-sitosterol (6.9 %) and n-Hexadecanoic acid (6.1 %) were the major contents in the wax.

## How to plant/cultivate
A plant of drier areas in the tropics. Thrives at higher light levels and lower humidity than most ferns, preferring bright, filtered sunlight.

## Commonly seen growing in areas
Sunny or lightly shaded conditions in rock crevices, In shallow soil pockets overlaying sheet rock.
