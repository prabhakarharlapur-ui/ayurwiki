# Viola biflora

[TOC]

![](../images/Viola_biflora_4.jpg)
**Viola biflora** is a herbaceous perennial plant forming a mat of growth 2 - 20cm tall from a short, creeping rhizome. The plant is harvested from the wild for local use as a food, medicine and incense. It is grown as an ornamental in gardens.

## Chemical Composition
Viola odorata contains alkaloids, glycosides, saponins, methyl salicylate, mucilage and vitamin C (Stuart, 1989). About 30 cyclotides are identified from the aerial parts and roots of Viola odorata, 13 of which are novel sequences (Ireland et al., 2006).

## How to plant/cultivate
Viola biflora is quite cold tolerant, unharmed by temperatures of -10°c and probably lower.

## Commonly seen growing in areas
Subalpine meadows, Thickets, Forest margins, Rocky crevices.
