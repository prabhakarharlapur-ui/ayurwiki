# Curculigo orchioides - Black Musli

[TOC]

![*Talamuli*, Curculigo orchioides
](../images/Kali_Musali_(Marathi-_काळी_मुसळी)_(2618279600).jpg)
**Talamuli** is a tiny herb that can be seen in the rainy season. They are deep rooted plants with palm like lance shaped leaves and yellow star shaped flowers.
## Uses
Piles, Biliousness, Fatigue, Blood related disorders, Bronchitis, Indigestion, Vomiting, Diarrhea, Lumbago, Gonorrhea, Ophthalmia, Gleet, Hydrophobia, Osteoporosis, Skin problems, Cold, Cough.

## Parts Used
Leaves, Stem, Flowers, Roots.

## Identification
### Leaf
Simple, Rosette, rhizome to 15 cm long, oblong, perennial. Leaves 10-15 x 2 cm, lanceolate, plicate, base sheathing, pilose, subsessile.

### Flower
Bisexual, Solitary, Yellow, 6, Flowering and fruiting is from January to September

### Fruit
Fruit baccate; seeds subglobose.

## Mode of Propagation
Tuber segment

## How to plant/cultivate
Tuber segments of 1.5–2 cm size, containing the apical bud, are collected during February–March and used for propagation.
