# Barringtonia acutangula - Samudraphala

[TOC]

![*Samudraphala*, *Barringtonia acutangula*](../images/Barringtonia_acutangula_(Freshwater_Mangrove)_fruits_in_Kolkata_W_IMG_8545.jpg)
**Samudraphala** is an evergreen tree. It's Sanskrit name is Hijja or Hijjala. The fruit is spoken of as Samudra-phala and Dhātriphala.
## Uses
Fever, Diarrhea, Diarrhea, Bronchitis, Splenomegaly, Headache

## Parts Used
Leaves, Roots, Fruits, Bark

## Chemical Composition
The bark contains tannins (16%), also ellagic acid. The fruits contain triterpenoid sapogenins.

## Habit

## Identification
### Leaf
Simple, Alternate, Alustered towards the tip of branchlets.

### Flower
bisexual, 6-8 mm long, Red, Many, terminal pendulous racemes.

### Fruit
Berry, 2.5-3.8 cm long, Oblong-ovoid, bluntly quadrangular; seed one, ovoid
