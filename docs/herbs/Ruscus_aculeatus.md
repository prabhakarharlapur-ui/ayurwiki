# Ruscus aculeatus

[TOC]

![*Ruscus aculeatus*](../images/Flora_della_Sardegna_133_(3).JPGthumb)
**Ruscus aculeatus** known as butcher's-broom is a low evergreen Eurasian shrub, with flat shoots known as cladodes that give the appearance of stiff, spine-tipped leaves.
## Uses
Haemorrhoids, Jaundice, Gout, Kidney stones, Bladder stones, Haemorrhoids, Hypertension, High blood pressure.

## How to plant/cultivate
Tolerant of most soils, including chalky and heavy clay soils. Prefers a shady position, tolerating dense dry shade and bad growing conditions, including the drip-line of trees. Dislikes much wetness at the roots. Established plants are drought resistant.

## Commonly seen growing in areas
Outskirts of dry woods, Moist uncultivated ground especially on chalk
