# Ipomoea marginata - Laksmana

[TOC]

![*Laksmana*, *Ipomoea marginata*](../images/Ban_Kalmi_(Hindi-_बन_कलमी)_(3034946290).jpg)
**Laksmana** is a slender climber with heart shaped leaves and violet coloured flowers, closely related to morning glories. It has long been used in Kerala and is one of the ten sacred flowers of Kerala. It is found throughout India in the plains, especially on the bank of stream and rivers.

## Identification
### Leaf
Simple, Alternate, Triangular-cordiform, 5-8 by 3-5 cm, apex acute-apiculate, base cordate, thinly pubescent, petiole 1-2 cm long.

### Flower
Bisexual, Cyme, Pinkish white, Cymes sub-umbellate. Sepals sub-equal, obovate, glabrous. Corolla pinkish-white, c. 3 cm across, salver-form. Stamens included.

### Fruit
Capsule, Globose, Seeds pubescent
