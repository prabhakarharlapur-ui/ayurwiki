# Ziziphus rugosa

[TOC]

![](../images/Ziziphus_rugosa_-_Wild_Jujube_flowers_at_Mayyil_2019_(8).jpg)
**Ziziphus rugosa** is a spiny, evergreen tree growing around 5 - 9 metres tall. The plant is harvested from the wild for local use as a food and a medicine.
## Uses
Swollen gums, Toothaches, Menorrhagia.

### Food
Ziziphus rugosa can be used in Food. Ripe fruits are eaten raw.

## Parts Used
Fruits.

## Chemical Composition
It contains lupeol, betulin, betulinic aldehyde, betulinic acid, alphitolic acid, euscaphic acid, zizyberenalic acid, and β−sitosterol etc.

## Mode of Propagation
Seeds, Division of root suckers.

## How to plant/cultivate
This species is an important host for the parasitic scale insect, Laccifer lacca (lac insect). Ziziphus rugosa is available through December to February.

## Commonly seen growing in areas
Riverbanks, Shorea forest, Sparse forests, Thickets on hills, Sunny places on mountains.
