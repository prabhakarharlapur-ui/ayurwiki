# Careya arborea - Kubhīkaḥ

[TOC]

![*Kubhīkaḥ*](../images/Kumbhi_(Marathi-_कुंभी)_(3362443430).jpg)
**Kulaµjana** consists of dried rhizome of Alpinia galanga. It is a plant grows up to about 2m tall. It is native to eastern Himalayas and southwest India. This plat is belongs to Lecythidaceae family.
## Uses
Body swellings, Cough, Cold.

### Food
Careya arborea can be used in food. Flowers cooked as vegetable and fruits are pickled.

## Parts Used
Fruit, Seed, Flower.

## Chemical Composition
Seeds contain triterpenoid sapogenols, sterols; leaves contain a triterpene ester, beta-amyrin, hexacosanol, taraxerol, beta-sitosterol, quercetin and taraxeryl acetate.

## Habit
Deciduous tree

## Identification
### Leaf
Paripinnate, Oblong, Leaf Arrangement is Alternate-spiral

### Flower
Unisexual, 2-4cm long, Pink, Flowering throughout the year and In terminal and/or axillary pseudoracemes

### Fruit
Oblong pod, Thinly septate, pilose, wrinkled, Seeds upto 5, Fruiting throughout the year

## Mode of Propagation
Seeds

## Cultivation Details
Fresh seeds germinate readily. Prefers a well-drained, sandy or even rocky soil. It is available through March to April

## Commonly seen growing in areas
Secondary forest, Slightly seasonal forest, Along forest edges.
