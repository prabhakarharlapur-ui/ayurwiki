# Gaultheria fragrantissima - Tailapatra

[TOC]

![*Tailapatra*, *Gaultheria fragrantissima*](../images/Gaultheria_fragrantissima_at_Mannavan_Shola,_Anamudi_Shola_National_Park,_Kerala_(1).jpg)
**Tailapatra** is an evergreen shrub, occasionally a small tree. It usually grows around 100 - 300cm tall, occasionally reaching 400cm. The plant is gathered from the wild for local use as a food, medicine and as a source of essential oil.
## Uses
Rheumatism, Pain relief, Intestinal worms, Fever, Inflammation

## Parts Used
Oil, Leaves

## Identification
### Leaf
Simple, Alternate, Leaves to 8 x 4 cm, elliptic, acute to obtuse at both ends, thick coriaceous, reddish gland dotted, aromatic, serrulate.

### Flower
Bisexual, Racemes, White, 10, Racemes to 8 cm long in upper axils.

### Fruit
Capsule, 8 x 6 mm, Cupular, blue with red stalk.

## Mode of Propagation
Seeds, Cuttings.

## How to plant/cultivate
Prefers a moist but not boggy humus rich soil in shade or semi-shade. A peat and moisture loving species, it requires a lime-free soil.

## Commonly seen growing in areas
Sub tropical area.
