# Costus speciosus

[TOC]

![*Costus speciosus*](../images/Starr_030807-0069_Costus_speciosus.jpg)

**Costus speciosus** is an erect, occasionally branched, herbaceous perennial plant growing up to 3 metres tall from a stout, creeping, tuberous rootstock.
The plant is harvested from the wild for local medicinal and food use. It is sometimes cultivated for medicinal purposes in India and is commonly grown as an ornamental in many areas of the tropics.
## Uses
Burning sensation, Constipation, Leprosy, Worm infection, Skin diseases, Fever, Asthma, Bronchitis, Anaemia.

## Parts Used
Powder of rhizome, Juice if the rhizome, Leaf.

## Chemical Composition
The rhizomes contain costusosides Saponins dioscin, gracillin and Beta-sitosteral, Beta-d glucoside

## How to plant/cultivate
Prefers a position in some shade in a moist, fertile soil. The plant has escaped from cultivation and become naturalised in a number of areas, spreading by means of its bird-dispersed seeds.

## Commonly seen growing in areas
Disturbed moist to wet thickets, Roadside banks at elevations.
