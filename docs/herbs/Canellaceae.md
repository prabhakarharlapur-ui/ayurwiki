# Canellaceae

[TOC]

![*Canellaceae*](../images/Canella_winterana_Guadeloupe2.JPG)
The **Canellaceae** are the family of [Flowering plant](Flowering_plant.md) in the order Canellales. Canellaceae is native to the Afrotropic and Neotropic ecozones.
## Uses
Wounds, Cuts, Snakebites, Curing liver disorders, Skin eruptions, Blotches, Pimples, Diarrhea, Sore throats

## Parts Used
Dried folaige, Whole herb.

## Chemical Composition
Monoterpenes are common, as are drimane-type sesquiterpenes, including cinnafragrins, cinnamodial, and capsicodendrin.

## Common names
| Language | Names |
| --- | --- |
| English | Agrimony |

## Habit
Tree

## Identification
### Leaf
Simple, Alternate, The leaves have a peppery taste, are alternate,spiral, or distichous in arrangement, simple, entire,coriaceous.

### Flower
Unisexual, The receptacles are barely excavated,and the hypogynous disc is absent

### Fruit
Clearly grooved lengthwise, Lowest hooked hairs aligned towards crown, With hooked hairs

### Other features
## List of Ayurvedic medicine in which the herb is used
* [Vishatinduka Taila](../medicines/Vishatinduka_Taila.md) as *root juice extract*

## Where to get the saplings
## Mode of Propagation
Seeds, Cuttings.

## How to plant/cultivate
Easily grown in most soils, preferring a calcareous soil. Thrives in a dry lightly shaded position, though it prefers full sun.

## Commonly seen growing in areas
Tall grasslands, meadows, Borders of forests and fields.
