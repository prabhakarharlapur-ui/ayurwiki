# Acacia senegal - Babula

[TOC]

![*Babula*, *Acacia senegal*](../images/Acacia_senegal_-_Köhler–s_Medizinal-Pflanzen-004.jpg)
**Acacia senegal** is a legume tree from the dry tropics and subtropics. It is valued for the production of gum arabic, the only acacia gum evaluated as a safe food additive. The leaves and pods are browsed by livestock.
## Uses
High cholesterol, Diabetes, Irritable bowel syndrome, Obesity.

## Identification
### Leaf
Bipinnate, Alternate, Leaves bipinnate, 3-8 pinnae (glands between uppermost and lowermost pinnae); rachis up to 2.5 cm long; pinnacles are pairs of 8-15, green; 2 stipular spines strongly recurved with a 3rd pseudo-stipular between them.

### Flower
Bisexual, Axillary spikes, Flowers yellowish-white and fragrant, in cylindrical, axillary pedunculate spikes, 5-10 cm long; calyx of each flower has 5 deep lobes, 5 petals and a mass of short stamens; pistil inconspicuous

### Fruit
Pods, 7.5 x 2 cm, green and pubescent when young, maturing to shiny bronze, often with dark patches and bearing prominent veins, seeds 3-6, smooth, flat, rather small, shiny, dark brown.
