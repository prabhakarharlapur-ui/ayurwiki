# Uncaria tomentosa - Uña de gato

[TOC]

![* uña de gato*](../images/Uncaria_tomentosa.jpg)

## Uses
Asthma, Arthritis, Diabetes, Fever, Crohns disease, Low blood pressure, Cholesterol, Depression, Anxiety

## Parts Used
Bark, Roots, Leaves.

## Chemical Composition
Most commercial preparations of cat's claw contain the plant species Uncaria tomentosa. Cat's claw is found in nature in two different chemotypes producing different alkaloidal constituents. Pentacyclic oxindoles are found in the roots of one type, while the tetracyclic oxindoles are present in the second type

## Identification
### Leaf
Simple, The leaves are bright green, simple, opposite, compound, and dimorphic, with small wide leaflets that are ovate or lanceolate when mature

### Flower
Unisexual, 3 inches long, Yellow, 5-20, Flowers Season is June - August

### Fruit
Linear and flat, 20 inches long, Containing oblong, winged seeds that are wind-dispersed, With hooked hairs

## How to plant/cultivate
A plant of mainly low elevations in the tropics, where it can usually be found at elevations up to 300 metres. Grows best in dappled shade

Typical of primary forest, Disturbed forest.
