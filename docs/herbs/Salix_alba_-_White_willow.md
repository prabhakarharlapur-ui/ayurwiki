# Salix alba - White willow

[TOC]

![*Salix alba*, *white willow*](../images/Salix_alba_leaves.jpg)
**Salix alba** is a species of willow native to Europe and western and central Asia. The name derives from the white tone to the undersides of the leaves.
## Uses
Fevers, Dyspepsia, Joint pain, Rheumatism, Arthritis, Gout, Immune diseases, Headache, Febrifuge

## Parts Used
Leaves, Inner bark.

## Chemical Composition
The inner bark of the White Willow(Salix alba L.)contains tannins, flavonoids, phenolic glycosides, and anti-inflammatory and fever-reducing salicylates.

## How to plant/cultivate
A very easily grown plant, it succeeds in most soils, including wet, ill-drained or intermittently flooded soils[21], but prefers a damp, heavy soil in a sunny position[22]. Rarely thrives on chalk[22] and dislikes poor thin soils[23]. Very tolerant of maritime exposure and atmospheric pollution.

## Commonly seen growing in areas
By streams and rivers, marshes and woods, wet fens on richer soils.
