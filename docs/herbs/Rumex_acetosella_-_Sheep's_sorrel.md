# Rumex acetosella - Sheep's sorrel

[TOC]

![*Rumex acetosella*, *Sheep's sorrel*](../images/Ahosuolaheinä_(Rumex_acetosella).jpg)

**Rumex acetosella** is a species of flowering plant in the buckwheat family Polygonaceae. The plant and its subspecies are common perennial weeds.

## Uses
fevers, inflammations, kidney and urinary tract diseases, intestinal parasites, eczema, herpes, itchy rashes, digestive problems, Sore throats

## Parts Used
Leaves.

## Chemical Composition
Crysophanic acid, and an iron-greening tannin. In the early stage of its growth, it abounds in oxalic acid

### Flower

### Fruit

## How to plant/cultivate
Most gardeners consider sorrel a perennial pesky weed. It is in good company, as many medicinal plants such as dandelion, plantain, and nettle are disdained as weeds to be rooted out of garden and lawn.

## Commonly seen growing in areas
Heaths and acid grasslands, A weed of acid soils, Borders of forests and fields.
