# Hydrastis canadensis - Goldenseal

[TOC]

![*Goldenseal*, *orangeroot*](../images/Hydrastis.jpg)
**Goldenseal** is a perennial herb in the buttercup family Ranunculaceae. and it is native to southeastern Canada and the eastern United States.
## Uses
Common cold, Fever, Digestive disorders, Stomachache, Peptic ulcers, Colitis, Constipation, Diarrhea, Hemorrhoids.

## Parts Used
Dried root, Rhizomes.

## Chemical Composition
The active ingredients of goldenseal include isoquinoline alkaloids, such as berberine, canadine, and hydrastine.

## Identification
### Leaf
Simple, Lobed, Simple basal leaf withers away by flowering time

### Flower
Unisexual, ½ to ¾ inch, Yellow, 5-20, Goldenseal flowers from June to July

### Fruit
Berry/drupe, ½ inch, Fruit is a round cluster, With hooked hairs, 2

## Mode of Propagation
Seeds.

## How to plant/cultivate
Goldenseal is somewhat difficult of cultivation, it prefers a good rich moist loamy leafy soil in shade or partial shade. Prefers a sandy, acid to neutral humus-rich soil

## Commonly seen growing in areas
Rich shady woods, Moist areas, Woodland edges, Deciduous forests, Clay soils
