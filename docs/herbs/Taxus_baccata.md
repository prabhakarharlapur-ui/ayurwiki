# Taxus baccata

[TOC]

![](../images/TaxusBaccataMallorca.jpg)
**Taxus globosa** is an evergreen shrub or a tree that can grow up to 4.6 metres tall, with specimens up to 15 metres reported in the highlands of Guatemala. The tree is sometimes harvested from the wild for local use as a food and source of wood. It is a potential source of the anticancer drug 'taxol'.
## Uses
Cancer.

## Parts Used
Fruits, Bark.

## Chemical Composition
It contains P-hydroxybenzoic acid (1), putraflavone (2), sequoiavone (3), 7, 13-dideacetyl-9, 10-debenzoyltaxchinin C (4), baccatin VI (5) and taxumairone etc.

## Mode of Propagation
Seeds, Cuttings of half-ripe terminal shoots, Air layering, Cuttings of ripe terminal shoots.

## Season to grow
Winter.

## Soil type required
Suitable for light (sandy), medium (loamy) and heavy (clay) soils, prefers well-drained soil and can grow in heavy clay soil. Suitable pH: acid, neutral and basic (alkaline) soils and can grow in very acid and very alkaline soils.

## Ecosystem/Climate
Taxus baccata grows best in the high humidity of mild oceanic climates.

## How to plant/cultivate
Taxus globosa is moderately cold hardy, tolerating occasional, short-lived temperatures falling to between -12.1°c and -6.7°c.

## Commonly seen growing in areas
Montane forest, Pine-oak forest.
