# Peucedanum ostruthium - Vietnamese mint

[TOC]

![*Peucedanum ostruthium*](../images/Peucedanum_ostruthium001.jpg)
**Peucedanum ostruthium** is a species of flowering plant in the family Apiaceae. It is native to the mountains of Central and Southern Europe but has been widely introduced outside its native range.
## Uses
Diabetes, Stomachache, Sexual desire, Dandruff, Inflammation, Cold, Flu, Diarrhea, Sore throats

## Parts Used
Leaves.

## Chemical Composition
Decanal, Dodecanol, Decanol, Alpha humulene, Beta Caryophyllene

## Identification
### Leaf
Simple, The leaves are divided into 3-6 toothed leaflets, with smaller leaflets in between

### Flower
Unisexual, 2-4cm long, Yellow, 5-20, Flowers Season is June - August

### Fruit
7–10 mm, Clearly grooved lengthwise, Lowest hooked hairs aligned towards crown

Vietnamese mint is a perennial and should be planted in a warm sunny position and keep the soil moist at all times.
