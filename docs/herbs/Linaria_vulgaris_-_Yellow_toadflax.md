# Linaria vulgaris - Yellow toadflax

[TOC]

![*Linaria vulgaris*](../images/Linaria_vulgaris_-_harilik_käokannus_Valingu.jpg)

**Linaria vulgaris** is a species of toadflax family and it is native to most of Europe, northern Asia, the United Kingdom, Spain, east to eastern Siberia, and western China.

## Uses
Wounds, Cuts, Snakebites, Curing liver disorders, Skin eruptions, Blotches, Pimples, Diarrhea, Sore throats

## Parts Used
Dried folaige, Whole herb.

## Chemical Composition
The ability of Linaria vulgaris (Scrophulariaceae) infusion to act as a scavenger of 2,2-diphenyl-1-picrylhydrazyl (DPPH) radical, reactive oxygen species (superoxide radical, hydroxyl radical, hypochlorous acid (HOCl)) and nitric oxide was investigated.

## Identification
### Leaf
Simple, Opposite, Stalkless. Blade linear, sharp-tipped, margins revolute.

### Flower
Unisexual, 25–30 mm, Orange, 5-20, Flower is Irregular (zygomorphic). Corolla yellow, fused, bilabiate, spurred

### Fruit
Simple, 7–10 mm, Clearly grooved lengthwise, Lowest hooked hairs aligned towards crown, Many
