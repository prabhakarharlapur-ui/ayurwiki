# Pupalia lappacea

[TOC]

![*Aloe Vera*](../images/Pupalia_lappacea_06.jpg)
**Pupalia lappacea** is a very variable plant. It can be annual or perennial, producing stems that can range in length from 50 - 200cm. Usually much branched, the plant is sometimes erect, but can also be prostrate and sprawling or even more or less climbing.
## Uses
Coughs, Diarrhoea, Oedema, Constipation, Boils, Cuts, Snakebites, Syphilis, Sore-throat, Flatulence.

## Parts Used

## Identification
### Leaf

### Flower
Ovate oblong, Greyish white, Bracts remain on the rachis after the flower falls. Flowering season is August-December

### Fruit

## Mode of Propagation
Seeds

## How to plant/cultivate
The plant is seen as a serious weed within its native range. The spiny, seed-containing burrs easily catch on passing animals, including people, and thus spread the plant to new locations.

## Commonly seen growing in areas
Savannah, Forest pathsides, Dry bushland.
