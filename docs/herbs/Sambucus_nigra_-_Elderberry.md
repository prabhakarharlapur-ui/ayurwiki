# Sambucus nigra - Elderberry

[TOC]

![*Sambucus nigra*, *Elderberry*](../images/Sambucus_nigra_004.jpg)
**Sambucus nigra** is a species complex of flowering plants in the family Adoxaceae and it is native to most of Europe and North America.
## Uses
Sinus infection, Cold, Flu symptoms, Blood sugar, Ease Allergies, Skin eruptions, Diabetes, Cancer, Acne, Skin rashes

## Parts Used
Fruits, Flowers.

## Chemical Composition
Elder flower contains flavonoids (up to 3%) composed mainly of flavonol glycosides (astragalin, hyperoside, isoquercitrin, and rutin up to 1.9%) and free aglycones (quercetin and kaempferol); minerals (8?%), mainly potassium

## Mode of Propagation
Seeds.

## How to plant/cultivate
Seed - best sown as soon as it is ripe in the autumn in a cold frame, when it should germinate in early spring. Stored seed can be sown in the spring in a cold frame but will probably germinate better if it is given 2 months warm followed by 2 months cold stratification firs.

## Commonly seen growing in areas
Wet and dry fertile soils, Primarily in sunny locations, Borders of forests.
