# Thymus serpyllum

[TOC]

![*Thymus serpyllum*](../images/20190228Thymus_serpyllum3.jpg)
**Thymus serpyllum** is an evergreen shrub that can grow up to 0.10 metres tall. It is harvested from the wild for local use as a food, medicine and source of materials.
## Uses
Bronchitis, Catarrh, Laryngitis, Indigestion, Painful menstruation, Colic, Hangovers, Alcoholism, Gum infections.

## Parts Used
Leaves.

## Mode of Propagation
Seeds, Cuttings of young shoots, Cuttings of half-ripe wood, Air layering.

## How to plant/cultivate
Requires a light well-drained preferably calcareous soil in a sunny position. Succeeds in dry soils. Grows well between stepping stones on paths, tolerating light treading.

## Commonly seen growing in areas
Dry stony ground, Open sandy heaths.
