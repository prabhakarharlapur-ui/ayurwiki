# Periploca aphylla

[TOC]

![**Periploca aphylla**](../images/Periploca_aphylla_2.jpg)
**Periploca aphylla** is a genus of plants in the family Apocynaceae. It is first described for modern science by Linnaeus in 1753. It is native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.
## Uses
Swollen joints, Cough, Flu, Ulcer.

## Parts Used
Flower, Stem.

## Chemical Composition
Periploca species revealed predominant metabolites, such as steroids, carbohydrates, terpenoids, phenylpropanoids, flavonoids, quinones, and aromatics.

## Identification
### Leaf
Simple, Ovate-oblong, 6-7 mm long, Pointed, Thick, Nerve obscure.

### Flower
Bisexual, Ovalate, Greenish outside, Dark purple, 1.2-2.5 cm long cymes on thick flower-cluster-stalks, fragrant.

### Fruit
Simple Fruit, Ovoid, 2.5 cm long, Seedpods are 5-10 cm x 5-6 mm, rigid woody, widely divergent, pointed. Seeds are 6-6.5 mm long, oblong, compressed.

## Mode of Propagation

## How to plant/cultivate
Periploca linearifolia usually forms a tangled mass on top of shrubs and small trees. It flowers mainly during the rainy season; fruits remain on the plant for an extended period of time. The average 1000-seed weight is 7.1 g.

## Commonly seen growing in areas
