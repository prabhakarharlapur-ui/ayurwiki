# Myristica fragrans - Mayaphala

[TOC]

![](../images/Myristica_Fragrans_-_ജാതിതൈ.JPG)
**Quercus infectoria** is a species of oak, bearing galls that have been traditionally used for centuries in Asia medicinally. Quercus infectoria or locally known as Manjakani in Malaysia

## Uses
Bleeding piles, Rectal bleeding, Irritable bowel syndrome, Liver disorders, Rectal prolapse, Uterine prolapse, Leucorrhea, Vaginitis, Toothache, Headache, Dysentery, Pimples, Cracked foot, Impotence, Toothache, Insomnia, Cough.

## Parts Used
Leaves, Jakayi.

## Chemical Composition
Gallic Acid & Tannic Acid are found in Manjakani

## Habit
Shrub

## Identification
### Leaf
Simple, Alternate, Estipulate; petiole 9-15 mm long, slender, grooved above, glabrous; lamina 9-18 x 2.5-6 cm, elliptic

### Flower
Unisexual, Yellow, 9-12, Male flowers: 3-5 in axillary cymes, urceolate; pedicels drooping; bracts deciduous; bracteoles persistent. Flowering from June to September

### Fruit
Fleshy capsule, 5 cm, Aril red, lacinate; seed brown, Fruiting from June to September

## Where to get the saplings
## Mode of Propagation
Seeds, Cuttings.

## How to plant/cultivate
Seed - best sown when fresh. Germination usually takes place in 5 - 6 weeks. Seed in the shell takes some 4 - 6 weeks to germinate.

## Commonly seen growing in areas
Evergreen forest, Western ghats
