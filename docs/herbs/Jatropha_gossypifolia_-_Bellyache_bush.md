# Jatropha gossypifolia - Bellyache bush

[TOC]

![](../images/Close-up_of_Jatropha_gossypiifolia_(4437643316).jpg)
**Jatropha gossypiifolia** is a much-branched, somewhat succulent, deciduous to evergreen shrub growing up to 3 metres tall.
## Uses
Venereal disease, Heart problems, Diarrhoea, Stomach ache, Indigestion, Thrush, Sores, Bruising, Swellings, Inflammations, Headaches.

## Parts Used
Leaf, Root, Seed.

## Chemical Composition
Jatropha gossypifolia contains the following constitunts Fatty acids, Sugars, Alkaloids, Amino acids, Coumarins, Steroids, Flavonoids, Lignans, Proteins, Saponins, Tannins, and Terpenoids

## Habit
Evergreen shrub

## Identification
### Leaf
Ovate, Shortly acute, Entire margins with a close row of shortly stipitate

### Flower
Capsule, 0.85cm long, Reddish, Reticulate-rugose. Flowering through out the year

### Fruit
Pods, Smooth and Oval, Contains 3-4 seeds, Fruiting through out the year

## How to plant/cultivate
A plant of the lowland tropics, often found in coastal areas. It is found in areas of Africa where the mean annual rainfall is between 400 - 1,200mm and there is a distinct dry season.

## Commonly seen growing in areas
Savannah woodland, Riparian areas, Monsoon vine forests, Coastal foreshores.
