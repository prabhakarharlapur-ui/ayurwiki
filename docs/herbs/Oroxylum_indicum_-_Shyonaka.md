# Oroxylum indicum - Shyonaka

[TOC]

![*Oroxylum indicum*, *Shyonaka*](../images/Oroxylum_indicum_W3_IMG_3170.jpg)
**Shyonaka** is a species of flowering plant belonging to the family Bignoniaceae. It is a tree which can reach a height of 12 metres.
## Uses
Wounds, Cuts, Snakebites, Curing liver disorders, Skin eruptions, Blotches, Pimples, Diarrhea, Sore throats.

### Food
Oroxylum indicum can be used in Food. Flowers are cooked as vegetable. Unripe fruits are pickled.

## Parts Used
Roots, Leaves, Fruits, Seeds

## Chemical Composition
Baicalein, oroxylin and pinostrobin along with one sterol, Stigmast.

## Habit
Tree

## Identification
### Leaf
Compound, Triangular, 2-3 pinnate, pinnae 5-9; imparipinnate, opposite; leaflets 3-5 in each pinnae, opposite, 7.5-15 x 4-9 cm, ovate, apex acuminate.

### Flower
Bisexual, 10cm long, Reddish-purple and pinkish-yellow, 5, Flowers in terminal racemes, large, reddish-purple and pinkish-yellow within

### Fruit
Flat, 30-90 cm long, Compressed, tapering at both ends, Seeds thin, papery, 5-6 cm long, winged all around except at base.

## Where to get the saplings
## Mode of Propagation
Seeds, Cuttings.

## How to plant/cultivate
Seed is the best propagation material and should be collected before split- ting of pods during February–March. Seed germination percentage is 80%–90% without any pretreatment. Oroxylum indicum is available through June to December.

## Commonly seen growing in areas
Tropical area, Borders of forests and fields.
