# Alstonia venenata - Vishagni

[TOC]

![*Vishagni*, *Alstonia venenata*](../images/Alstonia_venenata.jpg)
**Vishagni** is a small tree that can grow up to 8m. The leaves are arranged in whorls of 3-6 along the stem. Alstonia venenata is found in the hills of peninsular India and the [western ghats](western_ghats.md).

## Uses
Skin diseases, Epilepsy, Fever

## Parts Used
Leaves, Roots

## Chemical Composition
The plant is a rich source of indole
alkaloids. Major alkaloids in the stembark are alstovenine, venenatine, 3-dehydroalstovenine, reserpine (0.003–0.3%), venoxidine and kopsinine. The fruit contains vincadifformine type of alkaloids.

## Identification
### Leaf
Simple, Whorled, Leaves 12-20 x 1.2-3 cm, linear-oblong, acuminate at both ends, 5 at each node, lateral nerves many, very close.

### Flower
Terminal cymes, 20cm across, White, 5, Flowering and fruiting is from February to August

### Fruit
Hirsute, 6-8 cm long, Follicle 2-angled, acute., Seeds smooth
