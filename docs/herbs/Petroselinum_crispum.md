# Petroselinum crispum

[TOC]

![**Petroselinum crispum**](../images/Petroselinum_crispum_Neapolitanum_0zz.jpg)
**Petroselinum crispum** is a species of flowering plant in the family Apiaceae that is native to the central and eastern Mediterranean region. It is naturalized elsewhere in Europe and is widely cultivated as an herb, and a vegetable.
## Uses
Gastrointestinal disorders, Inflammation, Halitosis.

## Parts Used
Seeds, Leaves.

## Chemical Composition
Petroselinum crispum (Mill) flat leaves specimens were isolated and identified the flavonoids apigenin (1), apigenin-7-O-glucoside or cosmosiin (2), apigenin-7-O-apiosyl-(1 --> 2)-O-glucoside or apiin (3) and the coumarin 2",3"-dihydroxyfuranocoumarin or oxypeucedanin hydrate (4).

## How to plant/cultivate
Parsley grows best in moist, well-drained soil, with full sun. It grows best between 22–30 °C (72–86 °F), and usually is grown from seed.
