# Agastache scrophulariifolia - Purple giant hyssop

[TOC]

![*Korean mint *, *Agastache rugosa*](../images/Agastache_rugosa_02.JPG)
**Agastache rugosa** is an aromatic herb in the mint family, native to East Asia, China, Japan, Korea, Russian Primorye, Taiwan and Vietnam.
## Uses
Poor digestion, Fungal infection, Abdominal bloating, Indigestion, Nausea, Vomiting, Chest congestion, Diarrhea, Headaches.

## Parts Used
Leaves, Seeds.

## Chemical Composition
Seasonal variations in the chemical composition of leaves fromAgastache rugosa were analyzed by gas chromatography-mass spectrometry (GC-MS). Phytotoxic and antimicrobial activities of its essential oil were investigated by characterizing 31 components, primarily methylchavicol (80.24%),dl-limonene (3.50%), linalool (4.23%), 5-methyl-2-(1-methylethylidene)-cyclohexanone (3.84%), and β-caryophyllene (2.39%)

## Mode of Propagation
Seeds, Cuttings.

## How to plant/cultivate
Seed - sow spring in a greenhouse and only just cover the seed. The seed usually germinates in 1 - 3 months at 13°c

## Commonly seen growing in areas
Grassy places in mountains, Valleys, Less stony meadows.
