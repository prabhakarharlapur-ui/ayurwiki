# Lactuca serriola - Kahu

[TOC]

![*Lactuca serriola*, *prickly lettuce*](../images/Lactuca_serriola.jpg)
**Lactuca serriola** is an annual or biennial plant in the dandelion tribe within the daisy family. It has a slightly fetid odor and is commonly considered a weed of orchards, roadsides and field crops. Lactuca serriola is known as the compass plant because in the Sun the upper leaves twist round to hold their margins upright.[Western ghats](Western_ghats.md)
## Uses
Cardiac paralysis, Chronic catarrh, Cough, Swollen liver, Flatulence, Urinary tract infection, Ailments.

## Parts Used
Leaves.

## Chemical Composition
Acetylcholine chloride, atropine sulfate, carbachol, dicyclomine, dimethylsulfoxide, ethylenediamine tetraacetic acid, glucose

## Identification
### Leaf
Simple, Lobed or unlobed but not separated into leaflets and Leaf arrangement is alternate

### Flower
Unisexual, 2-4cm long, Blue to purple, and yellow, 5-20, The flower head has ray flowers only, meaning all of the individual flowers of the flower head have a strap-shaped ray

### Fruit
Simple, 7–10, At least a part of the plume is made up of fine bristles, Tuft or plume on fruit, Many
