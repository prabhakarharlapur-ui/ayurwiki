# Liquidambar orientalis

[TOC]

![](../images/Liquidambar_orientalis_arboretum_Breuil_1.jpg)
**Liquidambar orientalis** is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 10.00 metres tall. It is harvested from the wild for local use as a food, medicine and source of materials.
## Uses
Cough, Strokes, Infantile convulsions, Coma, Heart disease, Pruritis, Cancer, Scabies, Wounds, Ulcers.

## Parts Used
Leaves, Fruits, Bark, Roots.

## Mode of Propagation
Seeds, Cuttings of half-ripe wood, Suckers in early spring.

## How to plant/cultivate
Prefers a moist but not swampy loam in a sheltered position. Prefers a neutral to acid soil. Grows poorly on shallow soils overlying chalk.

## Commonly seen growing in areas
Flood plains, Marshy places, Valley sides near streams.
