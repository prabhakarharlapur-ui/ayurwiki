# Hybanthus enneaspermus

[TOC]

![Spade flower](../images/(hybanthus_enneaspermus)_shrub_at_Kambalakonda_02.JPG)
**Hybanthus enneaspermus** is a plant commonly found during the rainy season in dry regions with gravel and stones in the Deccan plateu of India. It can grow upto 60cm tall.
## Uses
Diarrhea, Urinary tract infections, Inflammation, Wound healing, Asthma.

## Parts Used
Barks, Flowers.

## Chemical Composition
The phytocompounds present in H. enneaspermus have been extensively investigated. The chief chemical constituents include alkaloids, flavonoids, steroids, terpenoids, phenols, dipeptide, isoarborinol, sitosterol, sugars and tannins.

## Identification
### Leaf
Simple, Alternate, Leaves subsessile, 0.6-3 x 0.3-1 cm, linear-lanceolate or elliptic-lanceolate, base attenuate, margins distantly crenate, crenations scabrous hairy, apex acute, hirsute; stipules 1-2 mm long, linear-lanceolate.

### Flower
Bisexual, Axillary, solitary, Pink-Purple, 5, Pedicel 0.6-1.5 cm long, slender. Sepals 5, subequal, 2-3 x 1 mm, lanceolate, acute, ciliate. Petals 5, pinkish, unequal; lower one 0.8-1.5 cm long, suborbicular, clawed, other 4 smaller, 3-5 mm long, elliptic or triangular-oblong. Flowering season is February-March

### Fruit
A capsule, 5 - 8 mm long, Seeds many, Ovoid, Fruiting season is February-March
