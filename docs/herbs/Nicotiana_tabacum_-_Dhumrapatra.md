# Nicotiana tabacum - Dhumrapatra

[TOC]

![*Dhumrapatra*, *Nicotiana tabacum*](../images/P1000485_Nicotiana_tabacum_(tobacco)_(Solanaceae)_Plant.JPG)
**Nicotiana tabacum** is an annual or short-lived perennial, erect. It can grow 70 - 250cm tall. The most widely grown tobacco species. It is a rich source of the alkaloid nicotine and has a long history of use as a medicinal plant and social stimulant.
## Uses
Joint pain, Skin diseases, Scorpion stings, Piles, Nausea

## Parts Used
Leaves

## Chemical Composition
The leaves of Nicotiana tabacum (tobacco) contain the toxic alkaloid, nicotine as a major constituent and several other pyridine alkaloids as minor constituents. Transdermal nicotine is now widely used as an aid to smoking cessation.

### Leaf
Simple, Alternate, Leaves alternate, decurrent on stems; leaf blade ovate to elliptic or lanceolate, 30(-70) × 8-15(-30) cm, membranous, glandular hairy, base narrowed, nearly half clasping, apex acuminate

## Mode of Propagation

## How to plant/cultivate
Seed - there is no dormancy, except that freshly harvested seed should be allowed a post-ripening period of 3 weeks. Surface sown, preferably in light shade, the seed usually germinates within 10 - 20 days at 20°c.
