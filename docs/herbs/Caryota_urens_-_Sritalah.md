# Caryota urens - Sritalah

[TOC]

![*Sritalah*, *Caryota urens*](../images/Caryota_urens_at_Mayyil_(1).jpg)
**Sritalah** is a large palm tree that can be found growing in the evergreen forests of the [western ghats](western_ghats.md) and planted freely throughout India. Traditionally the tree has been used to make toddy and starch is extracted from the inner stem.
## Uses
Arthritis, Burning sensation, Migraine, Snake bites

## Parts Used
Leaf, Stem Bark, Sap.

## Identification
### Leaf
Bipinnate, Alternate, 4-6 m long; pinnae 5-7 pairs, to 1.5 m long;  leaflets broadly cuneate, fan-shaped, 12-20 cm long, 7-10 cm wide at wider portion, raemorse at apex, many ribbed.

### Flower
Monoecious, Spadix, Yellow, Many, Spadix interfoliar, shortly peduncled, much branched, pendulous, to 4 m long; spathes few, 40-50 cm long. Flowering from October to March

### Fruit
2 cm across, Globose, reddish purple; seeds plano-convex, subreniform., Fruiting from October to March

## Mode of Propagation
Seeds

## How to plant/cultivate
Seed - At room temperature the seeds remain viable for 30 - 90 days, depending on storage conditions. An experiment in Sri Lanka on the effect of seed storage and exposure to sunlight revealed a germination rate of 99% for seeds sown after 30-day storage in a dark room. Seed germinate in 2-4 months. Seedlings can tolerate sun while quite small.
