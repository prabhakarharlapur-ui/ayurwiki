# Vitis coignetiae - Crimson glory vine

[TOC]

![*Vitis coignetiae*, *Crimson glory vine*](../images/Vitis_coignetiae_vine.jpeg)

**Vitis coignetiae** is a plant belonging to the genus Vitis that is native to the temperate climes of Asia, where it can be found in the Russian Far East, Korea and Japan. It was described botanically in 1883.

## Uses
Wounds, Cuts, Snakebites, Liver disorders, Skin eruptions, Blotches, Pimples, Diarrhea, Sore throats

## Parts Used
Fruits, Leaves.

## How to plant/cultivate
Prefers a deep rich moist well-drained moderately fertile loam[1, 200]. Grows best in a calcareous soil[200]. Succeeds in sun or partial shade though a warm sunny position is required for the fruit to ripen

## Commonly seen growing in areas
Tall grasslands, Meadows, Borders of forests and fields.
