# Artemisia pallens - Dhavana

[TOC]

![*Dried davana plant used as aromatic bouquet.*](../images/Davana-Dried.jpg)
**Artemisia pallens** is an aromatic herb in genus of small herbs. The flowers are racemose panicles, bear numerous small yellow flower heads or capitula, but the silvery white silky covering of down gives the foliage a grey or white appearance.
## Uses
Dry skin, Endocrine system, Cramping, Nausea, Fatigue, Pelvic pain

## Parts Used
Flowers, Leaves.

## Chemical Composition
Davana essential oil comprises of davanone, a major sesquiterpene ketone and other essential components such as linalool, dehydro-a-linalool, terpinen-4 oil, isodavanone, nordavanone, davanafurans, methyl cinnamate, ethyl cinnamate, bicyclogermacrene, davana ether, 2-hydroxyisodavanone, and farnesol.

## Habit
Traditional herb

## Identification
### Leaf
Simple, Alteranate, The leaves are grayish silver, each leaf is devided into smaller segment

### Flower
Unisexual, 2-4cm long, Yellow, 5, The Flower heads of davana are small and yellow. Flowering from March to April

### Fruit
Clearly grooved lengthwise, Lowest hooked hairs aligned towards crown, With hooked hairs, Fruting from March to April
