# Lycopus europaeus - Gypsywort

[TOC]

![*Lycopus europaeus*, *gypsywort*](../images/Wolfspoot_R0012816.JPG)
**Lycopus europaeus** is a perennial plant in the Lycopus genus native to Europe and Asia, and naturalized elsewhere.

## Uses
Gout, Cuts, Overactive thyroid, Liver disorders, Skin eruptions, Blotches, Pimples, Diarrhea, Sore throats

## Parts Used
Leaves

## Chemical Composition
It contains flavonoids, tannins, phenolic acid derivatives such as rosmarinic acid, a bitter essential oil and manganese, fluoride and other elements

## Identification
### Leaf
Simple, Opposite, Stalkless–short-stalked. Leaf blade lanceolate–narrowly ovate, hairy, margin large-toothed–pinnately lobed.

### Flower
Unisexual, 4 mm, Yellow, 2, Corolla almost regular (actinomorphic), with white and red dots, approx

### Fruit
Schizocarp, 7–10 mm, 4-parted schizocarp. Mericarps quadrangular, slightly flat, yellowish brown, with oil-secreting glands, Many

## Mode of Propagation
Seeds, Cuttings.

## How to plant/cultivate
Tolerates most soil types so long as they are wet. Grows well in shallow water.

## Commonly seen growing in areas
Europe and Asia, Marshlands, Wetlands.
