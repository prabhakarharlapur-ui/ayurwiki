# Pisum sativum - Vartula

[TOC]

![*Vartula*, *Pisum sativum*](../images/Doperwt_rijserwt_peulen_Pisum_sativum.jpg)
**Ksharaka** is P. sativum is an annual plant. It is a cool-season crop grown in many parts of the world. Planting can take place from winter to early summer depending on location. The average pea weighs between 0.1 and 0.36 gram. The immature peasare used as a vegetable, fresh, frozen or canned.
## Uses
Skin burn.

## Parts Used
Leaves, Fruits.

## Chemical Composition
It contains flavonoids, polypheols, alkaloids, glycosides, terpenoids, and saponins.

## Identification
### Leaf
Pinante, Alternate, Leaves even-pinnate, 25 x 10 cm, chartaceous, glabrous; leaflets 2-3 pairs, sub(opposite), ovate-oblong, 5 x 3 cm, base obtuse, margin coarsely toothed.

### Flower
Bisexual, Raceme, White, 5, Racemes to 10 cm, 2-3- flowered, or flower solitary. Calyx deeply 5-toothed, 1 cm long, glabrous; lobes oblong-acuminate, membranous.

### Fruit
A Pod, 8 x 1.5 cm, Glabrous; seeds subglobose.

## Mode of Propagation
Seeds

## How to plant/cultivate
Pre-soak the seed for 24 hours in warm water and then sow in situ in succession. Pea seeds germinate at an ambient temperature of between 4 - 24°c, with 13 - 18°c; sprouting should take place in about 7 - 10 days.

## Commonly seen growing in areas
Tropical area, Temperate area, Sub Tropical area.
