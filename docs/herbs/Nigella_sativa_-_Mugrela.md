# Nigella sativa - Mugrela

[TOC]

![*Mugrela*, *Nigella sativa*](../images/Nsativa001Wien.jpg)
**Mugrela** is an annual flowering plant in the family Ranunculaceae, native to south and southwest Asia.
## Uses
Indigestion, Stomach pain, Bloating, Colic, Menstrual disorders, Intestinal worms, Abscesses, Haemorrhoids, Orchitis

## Parts Used
Seed grains.

## Chemical Composition
The oil contains the carvone, d-limonene, and a carbonyl compound, nigellone. The seed oil has antibacterial, insecticidal, bronchiodilatory, hypotensive and immune-stimulant properties.

## Identification
### Leaf
Simple, Alternate, The leaves are compound and there is one leaf per node along the stem

### Flower
Unisexual, 2-4cm long, Yellow, 13, There are two or more ways to evenly divide the flower

### Fruit
General, 1.2 mm, The fruit is dry and splits open when ripe, Many

## Mode of Propagation

## How to plant/cultivate
Easily grown in any good garden soil, preferring a sunny position. Sow seeds in spring or early autumn in situ.

## Commonly seen growing in areas
Tall grasslands, Meadows, Borders of forests and fields.
