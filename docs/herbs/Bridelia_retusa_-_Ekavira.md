# Bridelia retusa - Ekavira

[TOC]

![*Ekavira*, *Bridelia retusa*](../images/Mulluvenga_(Malayalam-_മുള്ളുവേങ്ങ)_(2957650349).jpg)
**Ekavira** is a deciduous tree. Usually it grows up to 10 metres tall but occasionally to 20 metres. The bole is up to 30cm in diameter. Trees may or may not possess spines on the trunk.The tree is harvested from the wild for local use as a medicine, food and source of a good quality wood.

## Uses
Diarrhea, Dysentery, Arthritis, Diabetes, Hemorrhoids, Wound healing

### Food
Ekavira can be used in food. Ripe fruits are eaten raw.

## Parts Used
Bark, Leaves

## Chemical Composition
The bark contains 16–40% tannin. Presence of a triterpene ketone in the bark is reported. Ripe fruit
pulp contains beta-sitosterol and gallic and ellagic acids.

## Habit

## Identification
### Leaf
Simple, Alternate, 5-22 x 3-11 cm, broadly elliptic, oblong, elliptic-oblong.

### Flower
Unisexual, Axillary or terminal dense clusters, Greenish yellow, 5, Some times paniculate spikes often exceeding the leaves.

### Fruit
A drupe, 7 - 8 mm, Purplish-black, seated on the persistent, slightly enlarged calyx, pyrenes 2, epicarp crustaceous; seed one in each pyrene.

Acid treatment before sowing. Should be planted in the full sun; no aftercare is necessary. Ekavira is available through June to December.
