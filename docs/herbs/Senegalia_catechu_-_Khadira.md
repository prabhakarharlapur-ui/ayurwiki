# Senegalia catechu - Khadira

[TOC]

![*Senegalia catechu*, *Khadira*](../images/Khair_(Acacia_catechu)_leaves_&_fruits_at_Hyderabad,_AP_W_IMG_7263.jpg)
**Senegalia catechu** is a deciduous thorny tree which grows up to 15 m in height.

## Uses
Chronic catarrh, Chronic diarrhoea, Dysentery, Foliage, Skin eruptions.

## Parts Used
Flowers, Leaves.

## Chemical Composition
Catechin, catechu-tannic acid and tannin, Hydroxy benzoic acid, kaempferol, quercetin, 3,4',7-trihydroxyl-3', 5-dimethoxyflavone, catechin, epicatechin, afzelechin, epiafzelechin, mesquitol, ophioglonin, aromadendrin, and phenol

## Habit
Deciduous tree

## Identification
### Leaf
Bipinnate, Alternate, Leaves 2-pinnate, pinnae 40-80, leaflets 60-100, small ligulate

### Flower
Bisexual, 2-4cm long, Yellow, 5-20, Flowers small, pale yellow or white in cylindrical axillary spikes

### Fruit
Flat pod, 5-10 x 1-1.6 cm, flat, straight, unlobed or sinuate along margins, thin walled, beaked at apex, brown, narrowed at base into a stipe, dehiscent; seeds 3-10, orbicular or ovate, flattened.

## Mode of Propagation
Seeds, Cuttings.

## How to plant/cultivate
A plant for sub-tropical to tropical areas, tolerating a minimum temperature of about 7c. It is especially common in the drier regions, but can also be grown in the more humid climates of South-East Asia at elevations from sea-level to about 1,500 metres

## Commonly seen growing in areas
On well drained soil areas, Open areas, Drier areas, Sandy soil areas.
