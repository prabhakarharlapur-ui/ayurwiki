# Citrullus colocynthis - Indravaruni

[TOC]

![*Indravaruni*, *Citrullus colocynthis*](../images/Citrullus_colocinthus_habit_ii.jpg)
**Indravaruni** is a perennial plant with a woody, tuberous rootstock from which grow long trailing or climbing stems. The plant has long been valued for its medicinal uses and is cultivated in some regions for this purpose.
## Uses
Constipation, Fungal infection, Bacterial infection, Inflammation, Larvicidal, Cold, Cough

## Parts Used
Roots, Fruits

## Chemical Composition
Colocynth contains up to 3% cucurbitacin. The drug and its preparations cause drastic irritation of the gastrointestinal mucosa and haemorrhages.

## Identification
### Leaf
Simple, Alternate, Tendrils simple, rarely bifid, slender, glabrous.

### Flower
Unisexual, Axillary, Yellow, Male flowers on long pedicels

### Fruit
Pepo, 5 cm across, Globose, smooth, longitudinally green striped, 5-7 cm in diameter, eipcarp thin, filled with a dry spongy very bitter pulp, Seeds numerous.
