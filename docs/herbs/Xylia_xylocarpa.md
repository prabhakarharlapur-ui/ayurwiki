# Xylia xylocarpa

[TOC]

![](../images/Xylia_xylocarpa_-_Burma_Ironwood_flowers_at_Peravoor_2018_(3).jpg)
**Xylia xylocarpa** is a deciduous tree with a dense crown usually growing 20 - 40 metres tall. The bole is usually straight and cylindrical, sometimes with small buttresses. It can be unbranched for 12 metres or more and around 40 - 60cm in diameter.
## Uses
Worms, Leprosy, Vomiting, Diarrhoea, Gonorrhoea, Ulcers, Rheumatism, Piles, Leprosy.

## Parts Used
Fruits, Seeds.

## Chemical Composition
It contains The mature seeds contained 29.5% crude protein, 14.78% crude fat, 8.02% crude fibre, 5.11% ash and 42.6% crude carbohydrates. The seeds appeared to be a good source of potassium, magnesium, phosphorus and iron.
