# Cardiospermum halicacabum - Karṇasphoṭā

[TOC]

![*Karṇasphoṭā*](../images/Cardiospermum_halicacabum4.jpg)

**Karṇasphoṭā** consists of the root of Cardiospermum halicacabum. It is native to India, ascending upto 1200m in the North Western Himalayas. This plant is belongs to Sapindaceae family.
## Uses
Rheumatism, Nervous diseases, Stiffness of the limbs, Snakebite, Itchy skin, Swellings, Earache.

## Parts Used
Leaves.

## Chemical Composition
The leaves contain beta-sitosterol and its D-glucoside, an alkaloid, oxalic acid and amino acids.

## Habit
Tree

## Identification
### Leaf
Biternate, Oblong, Leaf are essentially 3-foliolate with each part divided again into 3 leaflets

### Flower
Unisexual, 2-4cm long, White with a yellowish centre, Sepals 4, Flowering throughout the year and In terminal and/or axillary pseudoracemes

### Fruit
Inflated capsule, 2 cm long, Capsule Globose, Not winged, Seeds upto 5, Fruiting throughout the year
