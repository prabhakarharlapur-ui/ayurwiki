# Tacca leontopetaloides

[TOC]

![*Tacca leontopetaloides*](../images/Tacca_pinnatifida_2.jpg)
**Polynesian arrowroot** is a perennial plant producing a single, leafy stem around 1 metre tall from a tuberous rootstock. The plant was at one time widely cultivated for its edible root in many areas of the tropics, especially in the Pacific Islands.
## Uses
Diarrhoea, Dysentery, Sores, Burns, Earache.

### Food
Tacca leontopetaloides can be used in Food. Tubers are repeatedly washed with water to remove bitterness and are then boiled and consumed.

## Parts Used
Roots.

## Chemical Composition
The chemical compositions of Tacca flour were 0.66 % total of nitrogen, 0.91% lipid, 0.05% ash and 85.7% starch content on dried weight.

## Mode of Propagation
Seeds, Division of rhizomes.

## How to plant/cultivate
A plant of low elevations in the moist tropics, where it is most commonly found near the sea and below elevations of 200 metres. Tacca leontopetaloides is available through July to October.

## Commonly seen growing in areas
Secondary forest, Thickets, Many open situations, Clearings, Grassland.
