# Verbascum thapsus - Great mullein

[TOC]

![*Verbascum thapsus*](../images/Starr_040723-0030_Verbascum_thapsus.jpg)
**Verbascum thapsus** is a species of mullein native to Europe, northern Africa, and Asia, and introduced in the Americas and Australia.
## Uses
Cough, Bronchitis, Asthma, Whooping cough, Insomnia, Hemorrhoids, Diarrhea, Sore throats

## Parts Used
Leaves, Flowers, Roots

## Chemical Composition
The flowers contain gum, resin, a yellow colouring principle, a green fatty matter (a sort of chlorophyll), a glucoside, an acrid, fatty matter; free acid and phosphoric acid; uncrystallizable sugar.

## Identification
### Leaf
Simple, Alternate, The leaves are lobed or unlobed but not separated into leaflets

### Flower
Unisexual, 2-4cm long, Yellow, 5, The flower is bilaterally symmetrical

### Fruit
General, 7–10 mm, The fruit is dry and splits open when ripe, -

## How to plant/cultivate
Typical growing conditions are full sun and mesic to dry soil that often contains clay or stony material.

## Commonly seen growing in areas
Temperate region, Himalaya region, The Channel Islands.
