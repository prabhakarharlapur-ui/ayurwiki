# Zanthoxylum armatum

[TOC]

![](../images/Zanthoxylum_alatum.jpg)
**Zanthoxylum armatum** varies in habit from a prickly, deciduous to evergreen shrub, woody climber or small tree growing up to 5 metres tall. The plant is mainly gathered from the wild for use as a condiment and medicine, as well as for various other commodities. It is sometimes also cultivated and is grown as a hedge in northern India.
## Uses
Fevers, Dyspepsia, Cholera, Abscesses, Arthritis, Bruises, Gastritis, Swellings, Toothache, Cuts, Wounds.

## Parts Used
Seeds, Young leaves.

## Chemical Composition
Chemical studies on the seeds of Zanthoxylum armatum (Rutaceae) led to the isolation of two new phenolic constituents characterized as 3-methoxy-11-hydroxy-6,8-dimethylcarboxylate biphenyl and 3,5,6,7-tetrahydroxy-3 etc.
