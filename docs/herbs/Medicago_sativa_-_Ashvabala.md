# Medicago sativa - Ashvabala

[TOC]

![*Ashvabala*,*Alfalfa*](../images/75_Medicago_sativa_L.jpg)
**Medicago sativa** is a perennial flowering plant in the pea family Fabaceae cultivated as an important forage crop in many countries around the world.
## Uses
Relieve water retention, Arthritis, Ulcers, Purifying the blood, Food digestion, Assimilation, Kidney problems.

## Parts Used
Leaves, Seeds.

## Chemical Composition
Tender shoots of alfalfa are reported to contain per 100 g 52 calories, 82.7% moisture, 6 g protein, 0.4 g fat, 9.5 g total carbohydrate, 3.1 g fiber, 1.4 g ash, 12 mg Ca, 51 mg P, 5.4 mg Fe, 3410 IU Vit A, 0.13 mg thiamine, 0.14 mg riboflavin, 0.5 mg niacin, and 162 mg ascorbic acid

## Identification
### Leaf
simple, dentate, The leaflets are 5-20 mm long and dentate (toothed) at the apex and sometimes at the base.

### Flower
pale lavender, 2-4cm long, violet, 6-12 mm long, The flowers are papilionaceous, typical of species

### Fruit
loose spiral, A curved or loose spiral seed pod is present, seeds are yellow to brow.n in colour, 10 to 20 seed

## Mode of Propagation
Seeds, Stem cutting.

## How to plant/cultivate
For cultivation of alfalfa, land should be well-plowed. Farm manure could be applied six weeks before planting, with additional manurings as top dressing after every third cutting. Crop is propagated by seed. As seeds have hard coat, they should be scarified or soaked in water before sowing.

## Commonly seen growing in areas
Western himalayas, Open woodland, Moisty soil area.
