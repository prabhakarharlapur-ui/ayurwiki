# Malus pumila

[TOC]

![*Malus pumila*](../images/Malus_pumila_Mcintosh_1zz.jpg)

**Malus pumila** is an edible fruit produced by an apple tree. Apple trees are cultivated worldwide and are the most widely grown species in the genus Malus. The tree originated in Central Asia, where its wild ancestor, Malus sieversii, is still found today.

## Uses
Intermittent, Remittent, Bilious fever.

## Parts Used
Fruits, Leaves.

## Chemical Composition
Nine compounds were isolated from the flowers of M. pumila, and identified as kaempferol-3-O-β-d-glucopyranoside, kaempferol-7-O-β-d-glucopyranoside, kaempferol-3-O-α-l-arabinofuranoside, phloridzin, kaempferol, phloretin, β-sitosterol, lupeol and pyracanthoside.

## How to plant/cultivate
The preference is full sun, moist to mesic well-drained conditions, and fertile loamy soil. The cultivated Apple is vulnerable to many insect pests and disease organisms, although some cultivars have greater resistance to them than others. Dwarf cultivars are available that produce full-sized fruit on trees less than 10' tall.

## Commonly seen growing in areas
Subtropical area, Meadows, Borders of forests and fields.
