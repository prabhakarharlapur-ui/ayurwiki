# Ficus religiosa - Pippala, Ashvatta

[TOC]

**Ashvattha** consists of dried bark of Ficus religiosa Linn. It is a large perennial tree, glabrous when young, found throughout the plains of India upto 170m
altitude in the Himalayas, largely planted as an avenue and roadside tree especially near temples. It's family is Moraceae.
## Uses
Haemoptysis, Fistula, Diarrhoea, Cholera, Wound healing, Scabies, Ulcers, Skin diseases.

## Parts used
Bark, Latex.
