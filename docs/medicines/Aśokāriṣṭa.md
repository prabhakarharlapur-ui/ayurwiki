# Aśokāriṣṭa

[TOC]

**Aśokāriṣṭa** is a fermented liquid preparation, made with the ingredients in Formulation composition given below. It contains not more than 10 per cent, and not less than 5 per cent of alcohol that is self generated in the preparation over a period of time.

## Method of preparation
* Take the raw materials of pharmacopoeial quality.
* Wash, dry and powder the ingredient numbered 1 ([Kvātha Dravya](Kvātha_Dravya.md)) of the formulation composition and pass through the sieve number 44 to obtain coarse powder.
* Clean, dry and powder the ingredients numbered 5 to 16 ([Prakṣepa Dravya](Prakṣepa_Dravya.md)) of the formulation composition individually and pass through the sieve number 85 to obtain fine powder.
* Add specified amount of water to the [Kvātha Dravya](Kvātha_Dravya.md), soak overnight, heat, reduce to one fourth and filter through muslin cloth to obtain [Kvātha](../Kvātha.md).

## Description
* Clear, dark brown liquid without frothing and significant sedimentation with astringent taste.

## Storage
* Store in a cool place in tightly closed amber coloured bottle, protect from light and moisture.

## Therapeutic uses
* Asṛgdara Ruja (Dysmenorrhoea), Yonirujā (Pain in female genital tract), Śvetapradara (Leucorrhoea), Jvara (Fever), Raktapitta (Bleeding disorders), Arśa (Piles), Mandāgni (Dyspepsia), Arocaka (Tastelessness), Meha (Polyuria), Śotha (Inflammation).

## Dose
* 15 – 30 ml orally with equal amount of water after meals twice a day.

## Anupāna
* Water.
