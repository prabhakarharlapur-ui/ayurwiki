# Parshvasana

[TOC]

**Parshvasana** is an Asana. It is translated as ***Side Stretch Pose*** from **Sanskrit**. The name of this pose comes from **parshva** meaning **side** and **asana** meaning **posture** or **seat**. This pose is a variation of Tadasana.

## Technique
1. Begin in Tadasana / Mountain Pose.
1. Inhale and move your left arm towards the ceiling while expanding your upper ribs.
1. Straighten your back and look at your palm.
1. Exhale and bend towards your right without disturbing your lower body.
1. Feel the stretch on the left side of your body.
1. Stick your right arm to the right side of your torso.
1. Stay in this pose for 3 long breaths.
1. Inhale and come into Tadasana / Mountain Pose.
1. Exhale and repeat the posture on your other side.

## Effects
* Reduces stress and fatigue.
* Stretches the sides, shoulders, arms and spine.
* Strengthens the abdominal muscles.
* Improves digestion.
* Improves flexibility and balance.
* Improves blood circulation.

## Special requisites
It is essential to practice this pose correctly to avoid injury.

* Anyone suffering from severe lower back, shoulder or neck injuries.
* Anyone suffering from headache or heart problems.
