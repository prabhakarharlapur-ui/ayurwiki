# Eka Pada Sirsa Uttana Pristhasana

[TOC]

**Baddha Natarajasana** is an Asana. It is translated as Bound Lord of Dance Pose from Sanskrit. The name of this pose comes from **baddha** meaning **bound**, **Nataraja** meaning **Lord of Dance**, and **asana** meaning **posture** or **seat**.

## Technique
1. First get into the position of Adho Mukha Svanasana (Downward facing dog pose) on the floor or yoga mat.
1. Keep your right foot in between your hands, your foot is little wider than your shoulders (for this place your foot heel-toe in the direction of the edge of the yoga mats).
1. After that, keep your hips towards forward and down.
1. Then, gently walk towards to your hands until it comes down onto your forearms. Now place your forearms on the floor along with keeping your chest open.
1. Now, slowly lengthens your spine and draw your shoulder blades together out of your back.
1. Place your right knee towards the middle line and look straight.
1. Maintain the pose around 30 to 60 seconds. After that release your position.
1. Repeat the same process with your alternate leg. Try to repeat the pose 3 times with your both legs; this will give you better output (result).
1. By practicing Utthan Pristhasana at least thrice (by both sides) will bring the better results.
